Tips for Effective Contact

Phone Calls

  • Be brief and specific. State your name, where you live, and the issue you're calling about.
  • Ask for a position. "Does the Senator/Representative support [specific bill or issue]?"
  • Share your story. Personal stories are powerful. Explain how the issue affects you or your community.
  • Be polite. Staff members keep track of constituent calls and report to the official.

Sample Phone Script

"Hello, my name is [Name] and I'm a constituent from [City/ZIP]. I'm calling to urge [Senator/Representative Name] to [support/oppose] [specific bill or action]. This issue is important to me because [brief personal reason]. Thank you for your time."

Writing Letters or Emails

  • Include your address. This confirms you're a constituent.
  • Focus on one issue. Don't try to cover multiple topics in one letter.
  • Be specific. Reference bill numbers if applicable.
  • Request a response. Ask them to reply with their position.
