Your vote is your voice. Whether you're voting for the first time or need a refresher, this guide will help you participate in elections.

Register to Vote

To vote in elections, you must be registered. Here's what you need to know:

Who Can Register?

  • U.S. citizens
  • Residents of the state where you're registering
  • At least 18 years old by Election Day (some states allow 17-year-olds to register)

How to Register

  • Online: Many states offer online voter registration. Visit your state's election website.
  • By Mail: Download and mail a voter registration form.
  • In Person: Visit your local election office, DMV, or other designated locations.
Register Online at Vote.org

Find Your Polling Place

On Election Day, you'll vote at your assigned polling place based on your address. Find yours before the election to avoid confusion.

Find Your Polling Place

What to Bring

  • Check your state's ID requirements - some states require photo ID, others do not.
  • Bring a utility bill or bank statement with your address as backup.
  • Know your rights - you can ask for a provisional ballot if there are issues.

Ways to Vote

In-Person Voting

Vote at your polling place on Election Day. Polls are typically open from early morning to evening.

Early Voting

Many states offer early voting days before Election Day. Check your state's early voting dates.

Absentee / Mail-In Voting

Request a ballot to vote by mail. Deadlines vary by state - apply early!

Your Rights at the Polls

  • You have the right to vote if you're in line before polls close.
  • You have the right to request assistance if you need help voting.
  • You have the right to a provisional ballot if your eligibility is questioned.
  • You have the right to report problems to election officials.

Report Voting Problems

If you experience issues voting, contact the Election Protection Hotline.

1-866-OUR-VOTE (1-866-687-8683)
